Mark Zuckerberg is an American business magnate, computer programmer, internet entrepreneur, and philanthropist. He is the co-founder, executive chairman, and CEO of Meta Platforms (formerly Facebook, Inc.), of which he is the controlling shareholder.

Zuckerberg was born in White Plains, New York, in 1984. He attended Harvard University, where he launched Facebook in February 2004 with his roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. Facebook quickly became one of the most popular social networking websites in the world, and Zuckerberg is now one of the richest people in the world.

In addition to his work at Meta, Zuckerberg is also the co-founder and co-CEO of the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, a philanthropic organization that aims to solve some of the world's biggest problems, such as curing disease and improving education.

Zuckerberg has been both praised and criticized for his work. He has been praised for his entrepreneurial spirit and his ability to build a successful company. However, he has also been criticized for his handling of user privacy and for his role in spreading misinformation on Facebook.

Despite the criticism, Zuckerberg remains one of the most influential people in the world. He is a visionary leader who is shaping the future of the internet and social media.
